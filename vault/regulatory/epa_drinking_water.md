# EPA Drinking Water Methods & Requirements

## Safe Drinking Water Act (SDWA) Compliance

### Primary Drinking Water Standards — Common Methods

| Contaminant Group | EPA Method | Description | MCL |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Metals** | EPA 200.8 | ICP-MS metals scan (As, Ba, Cd, Cr, Pb, Se, etc.) | Varies by metal |
| **Lead & Copper** | EPA 200.8 | Lead & Copper Rule monitoring | Pb: 15 µg/L (AL), Cu: 1.3 mg/L (AL) |
| **VOCs** | EPA 524.2 | Purge & Trap GC/MS | Varies (e.g., TCE: 5 µg/L) |
| **SOCs** | EPA 525.2 | Liquid-liquid extraction GC/MS | Varies |
| **Inorganics** | EPA 300.0 | Ion Chromatography (F, NO3, NO2) | NO3: 10 mg/L |
| **Disinfection Byproducts** | EPA 524.2 / SM 6232B | TTHMs, HAA5 | TTHM: 80 µg/L, HAA5: 60 µg/L |
| **Turbidity** | EPA 180.1 | Nephelometric | 1 NTU (95% of samples) |
| **Total Coliform** | SM 9223B | Colilert / presence-absence | Absent |
| **E. coli** | SM 9223B | Colilert | Absent |

### Lead & Copper Rule Revisions (LCRR)
- **Effective:** October 16, 2024
- **Key changes:**
  - Action level for lead remains 15 µg/L
  - New trigger level at 10 µg/L — requires additional corrosion control evaluation
  - Service line inventory required by October 2024
  - Tap sampling: first-draw and fifth-liter samples
  - Schools and childcare facility testing required

## EDD Format Requirements (EPA)
- Electronic reporting through EPA's Electronic Reporting Tool (ERT)
- Required fields: PWS ID, Sample Point, Collection Date, Analysis Date, Method, Analyte Code, Result, Units, MCL, Detection Limit
- Common errors:
  - Incorrect PWS ID format
  - Missing sample point identifiers
  - Non-detect results formatted incorrectly
  - Analysis date exceeding hold time from collection date
