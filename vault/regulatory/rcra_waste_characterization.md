# RCRA Waste Characterization Requirements

## RCRA Hazardous Waste Testing

### TCLP (Toxicity Characteristic Leaching Procedure) — EPA Method 1311

The TCLP test determines whether a solid waste exhibits the toxicity characteristic, making it a RCRA hazardous waste.

### Waste Characterization (Ignitability, Corrosivity, Reactivity)
| Characteristic | Test | Criteria |
|---|---|---|
| **Ignitability (D001)** | Flash Point (EPA 1010A) | Liquid with flash point < 140°F |
| **Corrosivity (D002)** | pH (SW-846 9045D) | pH ≤ 2 or pH ≥ 12.5 |
| **Reactivity (D003)** | Visual/narrative assessment | Unstable, reacts with water, generates toxic gas |

## Generator Classification
| Category | Quantity/Month | Accumulation Time |
|---|---|---|
| Very Small Qty Generator (VSQG) | ≤ 220 lbs (100 kg) | No time limit |
| Small Qty Generator (SQG) | 220–2,200 lbs | 270 days |
| Large Qty Generator (LQG) | > 2,200 lbs (1,000 kg) | 90 days |

## NC DENR Hazardous Waste Requirements
- Annual hazardous waste report due March 1
- EPA ID number required for all generators
- Manifest tracking for all off-site shipments
- Land Disposal Restrictions (LDR) notifications required
- Biennial reporting for LQGs
